NANA YAA ASARE BOADU
About
Nana Yaa Poku Asare-Boadu is a British born performance artist of Ghanaian heritage, who weaves a movement vocabulary of dance, speech, and video that complements and challenges histories of improvisation.

Deviating just so from dance-contact, Asare-Boadu considers how improvisational forces explore the self and relational entities both animate and inanimate. This repertoire of movement tests the possibilities of sensuality, with Asare-Boadu meandering between stoic and seductive postures that navigate how affect, audience, and architecture inform the physics of the black female body.

She has presented work at The Dreamhouse NYC, BRIC NYC, Soho house Los Angeles, and Dallas Museum of Art. Asare-Boadu lives and works between New York and Los Angeles. As well as performance, Asare Boadu also works as a movement director for music videos and fashion campaigns.
